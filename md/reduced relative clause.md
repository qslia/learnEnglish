In the sentence:

**There is no one *singing* in the room.**

**“singing” functions as a present participle used as a participial phrase modifying “no one”.**

### Breakdown

* **no one** = the noun (subject)
* **singing** = present participle
* **singing** describes what “no one” is *doing* → it acts like an adjective

### In grammar terms

* **Function:** *participle modifying the noun* (“no one”)
* **Role:** forms a **reduced relative clause**

### Expanded form

**There is no one who is singing in the room.**
⬇️ reduced to
**There is no one singing in the room.**

So **“singing” is a present participle modifying the noun “no one,” forming a reduced relative clause.**
